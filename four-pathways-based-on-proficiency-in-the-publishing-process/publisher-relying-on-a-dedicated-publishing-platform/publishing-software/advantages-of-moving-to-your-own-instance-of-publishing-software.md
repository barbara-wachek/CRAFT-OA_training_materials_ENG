# Advantages of Moving to Your Own Instance of Publishing Software

As a journal grows, its technological, organizational and image needs increase. Using shared infrastructure or off-the-shelf solutions offered by third-party operators may be convenient at the beginning, but over time it begins to limit an editor's capabilities. In such a situation, it is worth considering moving to your own instance of a publishing system, such as OJS (Open Journal Systems). Such a step gives you full control over your work environment and allows you to build solutions tailored to your journal's specific goals and ambitions.

### Advantages of your own instance:

📌 **Full control over the appearance and functionality of the system**\
Editors can independently modify the layout of the journal's page, add elements in line with the visual identity, customize the layout of content, interface languages or the way metadata is presented. This allows them to create a more professional and consistent image of the journal.

📌 **Free installation and configuration of plug-ins**\
Your own instance allows you to install extensions to support additional features, such as alternative rating systems (e.g., Open Peer Review), advanced statistics or integrations with DOI and ORCID systems. There is no need to wait for decisions from an external vendor or justify the need for changes – editors decide for themselves.

📌 **Integrations with external systems**\
The ability to connect custom data repositories, analytical tools (e.g., Matomo, Plausible), payment platforms (for journals with APCs) or author and reviewer identification systems. This makes publishing processes more automated, transparent and scalable.

📌 **Independence in updates and development**\
Editors can schedule updates and implement new features according to their own schedule. This is important when it comes to responding quickly to changes in the legal environment, database indexing requirements or user needs.

📌 **Adaptation of editorial processes to the specifics of the journal**\
A custom instance allows the system's workflow to be modeled according to the actual editorial process – from submission to publication - saving time, avoiding errors and increasing transparency.

📌 **More autonomy and prestige**\
An own IT infrastructure is also a signal to authors, reviewers and readers that the journal is organizationally and technologically mature. This can support internationalization processes, applying for grants or inclusion in reputable databases (Scopus, DOAJ, Web of Science).



### What to look for before making a decision?

Moving to an own instance also means responsibilities: the need to provide technical support, take care of data security, perform regular backups and schedule updates. The decision should be preceded by an analysis of resources – both human (e.g., whether the editors have access to a person with IT skills) and financial. It may be helpful to work with a parent institution, technology company or other journal that has experience in maintaining OJS or another system on its own.

It is also worth developing a strategy for the development of the system – what features are needed “at the start”, which will be implemented later, and how all this fits into the overall development plan of the journal.
